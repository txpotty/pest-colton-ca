Trustworthy Recognizing the Need for Raccoon Removal

Sometimes, it's not immediately obvious that you have a raccoon problem. However, these animals often leave behind clues that indicate their presence. Recognizing these signs early can help you address the issue before it escalates into a more significant infestation or causes extensive damage. Being vigilant and knowing what to look for is the first step in protecting your property.

Common Signs of Raccoon Activity

Keep an eye out for these tell-tale indicators that raccoons might be sharing your property:

  • Noises at Night: Raccoons are primarily nocturnal, meaning they are most active after dark. If you hear scratching, thumping, chattering, or crying sounds coming from your attic, walls, chimney, or crawl space during the night, it's a strong possibility that raccoons are present. Baby raccoons, called kits, often make distinct crying or whimpering sounds. These noises can be quite unsettling and disruptive to your sleep.
  • Damaged Trash Cans: Raccoons are notorious for raiding garbage cans in search of food. If you find your trash cans consistently overturned, lids removed, and garbage scattered around, it's a classic sign of raccoon foraging. They are intelligent and persistent when it comes to accessing food sources. Secure lids and sturdy containers are often no match for a determined raccoon.
  • Disturbed Gardens or Lawns: Raccoons may dig up lawns or gardens in search of grubs, insects, or other food sources. If you notice unexplained patches of dug-up earth or damaged plants, raccoons could be the culprits. Their digging can cause unsightly damage to your landscaping. They are opportunistic feeders and will explore various areas for sustenance.
  • Damage to Property Exterior: Look for physical damage to the exterior of your home or business. This can include torn screens, damaged vents, broken shingles, bent flashing, or holes in siding or fascia boards. Raccoons will chew, tear, and push their way into buildings if they find a weak spot. These entry points not only allow raccoons in but can also lead to water damage and other issues.
  • Animal Droppings: Raccoon feces can be found in specific locations they frequent, often in latrines. These droppings can be a health hazard. It's important never to handle animal droppings without proper protection and knowledge of how to identify them. Raccoon droppings are typically tubular and may contain remnants of what they've eaten.
  • Missing Pet Food or Water: If you leave pet food or water bowls outside, you might find them empty or disturbed by raccoons searching for an easy meal. Securing pet food is an important part of preventing wildlife attraction. Even small amounts of accessible food can entice them to your property.
  • Unusual Odors: Raccoon activity can create unpleasant odors, particularly from their urine and feces. If you notice strong, musky, or unpleasant smells emanating from areas like your attic or crawl space, it could be a sign of a raccoon infestation. These odors can permeate throughout your home and become quite strong over time.
  • Footprints or Tracks: In soft ground, mud, or dust, you might find footprints. Raccoon tracks are distinctive and resemble small human handprints with five toes. Look for tracks near potential entry points or areas where you suspect activity. Identifying tracks can help confirm the presence of raccoons.

Why These Signs Matter

Ignoring these signs can lead to a worsening problem and potentially more significant issues down the line. Here's why it's important to address raccoon activity promptly:

Potential Health Risks

Raccoons can carry various diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans and pets. Rabies is a serious concern, although less common. Raccoon roundworm is another significant health risk, as the eggs can be present in their feces and are easily inhaled or ingested. Leptospirosis is another bacterial disease raccoons can carry. These health risks underscore the importance of professional removal and sanitation. Protecting your family's health is paramount.

Property Damage

Raccoons can cause extensive and costly damage to your home or business. They can tear apart insulation for nesting material, chew on electrical wires (creating a fire hazard), damage roofing and siding to gain entry, and contaminate areas with their waste. The longer they are present, the more damage they can inflict. The cost of repairs can quickly add up, making early intervention a wise financial decision.

Aggression and Danger

While generally not aggressive towards humans, raccoons can become defensive, especially if they feel threatened or if they are protecting their young. Approaching or attempting to handle a raccoon yourself can be dangerous. Professional wildlife removal specialists have the expertise and equipment to handle these animals safely and minimize risks. It's always best to leave wildlife handling to the professionals.

Rapid Reproduction

Raccoons can reproduce quickly, and a small problem can escalate into a larger infestation if not addressed in a timely manner. A female raccoon can have a litter of several kits, and these young will grow and potentially remain in the area. Addressing the problem early is crucial for preventing exponential growth of the raccoon population on your property. Colton Pest Pros understands the reproductive cycles of local wildlife.

Attraction of Other Pests

Raccoon feces, urine, and nesting materials can attract other pests and insects to your property, creating a secondary infestation. The presence of one type of pest can often lead to others being attracted by the conditions created. For example, insects might be drawn to the waste, or rodents might be attracted to food sources raccoons have disturbed. Our services, like cockroach control or ant control, can address secondary issues.

If you've noticed any of these signs, don't delay seeking professional help. Early intervention is the key to resolving raccoon issues effectively and preventing further problems. We can help you determine the extent of the issue and implement the right solutions.

After a raccoon is captured, it is safely transported by our trained technicians to a designated relocation site. How can I prevent raccoons from returning after removal?

Prevention is a key component of our service. After removing the raccoons, we focus on exclusion methods to seal off all potential entry points into your home or business. This includes repairing damaged areas, reinforcing vulnerable spots, and installing barriers like chimney caps and vent covers. We will also provide you with advice on making your property less attractive to raccoons, such as securing trash cans with tight-fitting lids, removing outdoor pet food and water dishes at night, and trimming tree branches that provide easy access to your roof. Implementing these preventative measures is crucial for long-term success. Without proper exclusion, raccoons or other wildlife, perhaps even squirrels or rodents, could simply find their way back in.

Are raccoons dangerous to people or pets?

Raccoons can pose potential risks to both humans and pets. They can carry diseases such as rabies, raccoon roundworm, and leptospirosis, which can be transmitted through bites, scratches, or contact with their feces. While not inherently aggressive, raccoons can become defensive if cornered or if they perceive a threat, especially when protecting their young. It's essential to maintain a safe distance and never attempt to pet or feed wild raccoons. Keeping your pets' vaccinations up to date is also important in case of an encounter. Our professional handling minimizes any risk during the removal process.

What kind of damage can raccoons cause in attics?

Attics are common nesting sites for raccoons, and their presence there can lead to significant damage. They can tear up insulation for nesting material, which reduces your home's energy efficiency. They may chew on electrical wiring, creating a serious fire hazard. Raccoon feces and urine can soil insulation and drywall, causing unpleasant odors and potential health risks from airborne pathogens. The weight of raccoons and their nests can also damage ceilings. Our service includes inspecting attics and addressing damage caused by raccoons. This type of damage can be quite extensive and goes beyond just the nuisance of having them there.

Are there times of the year when raccoon problems are more common?

Raccoon activity can occur year-round, but certain times may see an increase in issues. Spring is often a busy time as female raccoons look for safe places to give birth and raise their young. Attics, chimneys, and sheds are attractive den sites during this period. Late summer and fall can also see increased activity as young raccoons become more independent and forage for food before winter. Understanding these seasonal patterns helps us anticipate and address potential problems. Our wildlife control services are available throughout the year.

Our exclusion process is designed to be thorough and long-lasting. We don't just patch holes; we reinforce vulnerable areas using sturdy materials that raccoons cannot easily chew or push through. We assess the entire property for potential entry points, even those that might seem too small to you, as raccoons are very flexible. Our goal is to create a comprehensive barrier that prevents any future access to your building. This meticulous approach to sealing is what truly solves the long-term problem.
